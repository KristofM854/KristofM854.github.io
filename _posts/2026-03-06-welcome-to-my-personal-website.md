Welcome to my personal website. I am Kristof Moeller — a marine biochemist and data scientist working at the IAEA Marine Environment Laboratories in Monaco, where I focus on harmful algal blooms, marine biotoxins, and the monitoring systems that underpin food safety and ocean governance. This site collects my professional work, projects, and writing in one place.

## What you will find here

My [publications](/publications/) list all seven of my peer-reviewed papers, with full author strings, journal links, and DOIs. My research spans harmful algal bloom ecology, marine biotoxin detection by LC-MS/MS, and toxicological risk assessment of microalgal species.

I completed my doctorate at the Alfred-Wegener-Institute in Bremerhaven in 2024, and have been based at IAEA-MEL in Monaco since late 2024 as an Associate Research Scientist.

This [blog](/blog/) is where I write longer-form reflections — on science, fieldwork, data analysis, and what it means to work at the boundary of research and international policy.

If you are interested in consulting, expert advisory work, or speaking invitations, the [contact page](/contact/) has the relevant details. I am available for engagements in harmful algal bloom risk assessment, marine biotoxin monitoring programme design, and environmental data analysis.

More posts to follow. Thanks for stopping by.
