Kristof Moeller

Marine Biochemist | Harmful Algal Blooms & Biotoxins | Environmental Risk & Marine Monitoring at the IAEA Marine Environment Laboratories

I'm a biochemist working at the intersection of marine science, environmental monitoring, and public health, currently based at the IAEA Marine Environment Laboratories in Monaco. My work focuses on harmful algal blooms (HABs), marine biotoxins, and improving monitoring systems that support food safety and policy response.

My background combines hands-on fieldwork, lab expertise (especially in analytical chemistry), and data analysis in R. I've participated in and contributed to the organization and execution of research cruises, worked with international teams, and contributed to studies that aim to manage and mitigate harmful algal blooms.

I specialize in applying scientific tools to real-world environmental challenges. Whether it's improving early warning systems for algal toxins, supporting regulatory frameworks, or training stakeholders, I'm motivated by science that leads to practical, equitable impact.

I'm especially interested in roles that connect marine environmental research with policy design, program coordination, or environmental consulting, especially in the context of international cooperation and sustainable ocean governance.
